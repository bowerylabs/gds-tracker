Methodology

A backward-looking, quarterly composite across three observable governance dimensions. All data sourced from public records. Judgment calls are logged below.

GDS = (PUCCnorm × 0.25) + (TAInorm × 0.25) + (ESnorm × 0.35) + (CIInorm × 0.15)
v1.1 — CII added to capture commitment rollbacks. A lab that removes a formal safety commitment is penalised, not treated the same as one that never made it. ES remains highest weight as the only metric measuring governance in practice.
25%

PUCC — Prohibited Use Category Count

Count of distinct high-risk use categories explicitly named in each lab's usage / acceptable use policy. Normalized against a theoretical max of 15. Tracks additions and deletions each quarter.

25%

TAI — Transparency Artifact Index

Binary 0–5 checklist per lab per quarter: system card exists, risk classification table present, red-team methodology disclosed, limitations documented, updated within 12 months. Scored on the most recently released production model.

35%

ES — Enforcement Signal

0–4 composite: disclosure of any enforcement data (1pt), quantified volume or rate (1pt), published within 12 months (1pt), breakdown by violation category (1pt). Highest weight: cannot be improved by communications alone.

15%

CII — Commitment Integrity Index

0–4 composite tracking whether formal public safety commitments are honoured. Rollbacks reduce the score; reinstatement restores it. A lab that removes a commitment scores lower than one that never made it.

PUCC — Keyword Taxonomy

The following categories form the master taxonomy. Scoring counts at the most granular level named in each policy document.

  • Bioweapons / biological weapons
  • Chemical weapons
  • Radiological or nuclear weapons
  • CSAM / child sexual abuse material
  • Election interference / influence operations
  • Autonomous lethal weapons / autonomous weapons systems
  • Mass surveillance
  • Cyberweapons / malware / offensive cyber
  • Human trafficking
  • Self-harm facilitation
  • Disinformation / synthetic media for deception
  • Critical infrastructure attacks
  • Non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII)
  • Personalised professional advice without oversight (legal, medical, financial)
  • Censorship / government surveillance tools
TAI — Checklist Elements
  • (1pt) A public system card or model card exists for the most recently released production model.
  • (1pt) The card includes an explicit risk or harm classification table or taxonomy.
  • (1pt) Red-team or adversarial testing methodology is disclosed — not just "we red-teamed" but some description of scope.
  • (1pt) Known limitations or failure modes are explicitly listed.
  • (1pt) The card has been published or updated within 12 months of the scoring date.
ES — Scoring Components
  • Disclosure (1pt): The lab publishes any enforcement data — transparency reports, usage policy enforcement statistics, or account action summaries.
  • Volume/rate (1pt): Published data includes a quantified count or rate of enforcement actions, not just qualitative statements.
  • Recency (1pt): Enforcement data has been published within the last 12 months of the scoring date.
  • Granularity bonus (1pt): Data breaks enforcement down by violation category (e.g., X% weapons-related, Y% CSAM) rather than aggregate totals only.
CII — Commitment Integrity Index (added v1.1)

Each lab is scored 0–4 each quarter based on whether four categories of formal public safety commitment are actively maintained. A commitment that is made and then retracted scores 0 — lower than a lab that never made the commitment. Reinstatement in a later quarter restores the point.

  • Safety evaluations (1pt): Lab maintains a formal, documented pre-deployment evaluation regime for frontier models — equivalent to an RSP, Preparedness Framework, or Frontier Safety Framework — that specifies concrete capability thresholds or "red lines." Source: Anthropic RSP v3.0; OpenAI Preparedness Framework v2; Google Frontier Safety Framework v3.
  • Transparency commitments (1pt): Lab maintains active commitments to publish system cards / model cards for all production models prior to or at release, as pledged under the Frontier AI Safety Commitments (Bletchley Park, 2023) or equivalent. A release without a card in a given quarter triggers a 0 for that quarter.
  • External oversight (1pt): Lab participates in a third-party safety review, independent audit mechanism, or government-recognised safety testing programme. Labs that explicitly refused or declined such mechanisms score 0. Meta's absence of a formal RSP-equivalent by the Feb 2025 Bletchley deadline scores 0 throughout.
  • Deployment restrictions (1pt): Lab maintains at least one formally stated, specific deployment threshold or capability-based deployment restriction (e.g., a pause commitment, a "Critical" capability trigger, or a hard model-size threshold). Removal of a previously stated restriction reduces the score immediately. Anthropic's RSP v3.0 (Feb 2026) removed the training-pause commitment — the first documented rollback in the dataset.

RSP equivalents tracked: Anthropic Responsible Scaling Policy (v1 Jun 2023 → v3.0 Feb 2026) · OpenAI Preparedness Framework (v1 Dec 2023 → v2 Apr 2025) · Google Frontier Safety Framework (v1 May 2024 → v3 Mar 2026) · Meta: no formal RSP equivalent published.

Forward Weight Schedule

As enforcement disclosure norms improve under regulatory pressure, ES weight is expected to increase. CII weight may grow as commitment rollbacks become a more discriminating signal.

  • v1.0 (2024–2025): PUCC 0.30 · TAI 0.30 · ES 0.40 — original three-factor formula
  • v1.1 (Q1 2026, current): PUCC 0.25 · TAI 0.25 · ES 0.35 · CII 0.15 — CII added to capture commitment rollbacks
  • v2.0 (2027, planned): PUCC 0.20 · TAI 0.20 · ES 0.45 · CII 0.15 — ES elevated as disclosure norms improve
  • v3.0 (2028+, planned): PUCC 0.15 · TAI 0.20 · ES 0.50 · CII 0.15 — ES majority weight when quantitative enforcement data is routine
