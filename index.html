Methodology

A backward-looking, quarterly composite across three observable governance dimensions. All data sourced from public records. Judgment calls are logged below.

GDS = (PUCCnorm × 0.30) + (TAInorm × 0.30) + (ESnorm × 0.40)
ES carries highest weight: it is the only metric that measures governance in practice, not governance on paper — and the hardest to game.
30%

PUCC — Prohibited Use Category Count

Count of distinct high-risk use categories explicitly named in each lab's usage / acceptable use policy. Normalized against a theoretical max of 15. Tracks additions and deletions each quarter.

30%

TAI — Transparency Artifact Index

Binary 0–5 checklist per lab per quarter: system card exists, risk classification table present, red-team methodology disclosed, limitations documented, updated within 12 months. Scored on the most recently released production model.

40%

ES — Enforcement Signal

0–4 composite: disclosure of any enforcement data (1pt), quantified volume or rate (1pt), published within 12 months (1pt), breakdown by violation category (1pt). Most important sub-score — highest weight precisely because it cannot be improved by communications alone.

PUCC — Keyword Taxonomy

The following categories form the master taxonomy. Scoring counts at the most granular level named in each policy document.

  • Bioweapons / biological weapons
  • Chemical weapons
  • Radiological or nuclear weapons
  • CSAM / child sexual abuse material
  • Election interference / influence operations
  • Autonomous lethal weapons / autonomous weapons systems
  • Mass surveillance
  • Cyberweapons / malware / offensive cyber
  • Human trafficking
  • Self-harm facilitation
  • Disinformation / synthetic media for deception
  • Critical infrastructure attacks
  • Non-consensual intimate imagery (NCII)
  • Personalised professional advice without oversight (legal, medical, financial)
  • Censorship / government surveillance tools
TAI — Checklist Elements
  • (1pt) A public system card or model card exists for the most recently released production model.
  • (1pt) The card includes an explicit risk or harm classification table or taxonomy.
  • (1pt) Red-team or adversarial testing methodology is disclosed — not just "we red-teamed" but some description of scope.
  • (1pt) Known limitations or failure modes are explicitly listed.
  • (1pt) The card has been published or updated within 12 months of the scoring date.
ES — Scoring Components
  • Disclosure (1pt): The lab publishes any enforcement data — transparency reports, usage policy enforcement statistics, or account action summaries.
  • Volume/rate (1pt): Published data includes a quantified count or rate of enforcement actions, not just qualitative statements.
  • Recency (1pt): Enforcement data has been published within the last 12 months of the scoring date.
  • Granularity bonus (1pt): Data breaks enforcement down by violation category (e.g., X% weapons-related, Y% CSAM) rather than aggregate totals only.
